2, My Sentiments

Next, I tallied the count of each emotion.

sentiCount = {'sadness':0, 'happiness':0, 'like':0, 'anger':0, 'surprise':0, 'disgust':0, 'fear':0, 'none':0}

for i,j in res.items():
  for m,n in j.items():
    if type(n) == list:
      for k in n:
        sentiCount[k]+=1
    else:
      sentiCount[n]+=1
    
print(sentiCount)

3Ôºå Some Basic Concepts

In today‚Äôs AI-booming era, operations have gradually become simplified, allowing for direct model usage without the need for extensive explanations. Most people can quickly get involved and learn about their interests in AI. AI drawing even has open-source graphical interfaces, eliminating the need for even basic programming languages like Python. Thus, everything seems simple (at least if you don‚Äôt delve too deep, perhaps).

Back to sentiment analysis, one could actually train a classifier themselves (SVM, Logistic Regression, etc.), and the results might not necessarily be worse than those of so-called deep learning models. Frankly, if there‚Äôs an appropriate dataset, hard training might yield even better results.

Indeed, I prefer to discuss conceptual stuff later on, as starting with all sorts of technical jargon can be headache-inducing.

  • Transformer: An encoder-decoder architecture that shone brightly after being mentioned in Attention is All You Need.
  • Encoder: The Encoder understands the content of an article and passes the information to the Decoder.
  • Decoder: Generates corresponding tasks using the results given by the Encoder.
  • BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers): The encoder part of a transformer, usually used for article classification, named entity recognition, and other tasks.
  • GPT (Generative Pretrained Transformer): In contrast to BERT, it uses only the decoder part, typically for generative tasks.
